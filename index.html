Success Stories

Real stories from individuals and communities who have transformed their lives through our microfinance programs.

Ibrahim's Sorghum Revolution

After receiving a microloan of $750, Ibrahim was able to invest in drought-resistant sorghum seeds and a small irrigation system. Within two growing seasons, he increased his yield by 300% and has now expanded to employ three other community members.

Ibrahim Mahmoud | Farmer, Aroma District

Amina's Textile Cooperative

Starting with just three sewing machines and a group of five women, Amina's cooperative accessed financing to purchase materials in bulk. Today, their textiles are sold in markets across eastern Sudan, and the cooperative has grown to include 23 women.

Amina Osman | Cooperative Leader, Kassala City

Mohammed's Tech Repair Business

At just 22 years old, Mohammed used his youth entrepreneurship loan to open a mobile phone repair shop. After completing our technical training program, he now services over 200 devices monthly and has hired two apprentices from his community.

Mohammed Ali | Young Entrepreneur, New Halfa

Wad Al-Hilew Irrigation Cooperative

Facing persistent water shortages, 17 farming families formed a cooperative to install a shared solar-powered irrigation system. With financing and technical support from our foundation, crop yields have increased by 70% across all member farms.

Wad Al-Hilew Cooperative | Rural Kassala
